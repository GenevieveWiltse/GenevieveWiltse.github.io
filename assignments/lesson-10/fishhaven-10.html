Cloudy with a Chance of Rainbows

by Genevieve Wiltse

The rain didn't put a damper on the annual Farmer's Winter Fest this weekend. Shortly after a much needed hard downpour, the skies parted and a beautiful double rainbow appeared. Festivities continued as scheduled and the indoor/outdoor event brought people from all over. Crafts and desserts were displayed and sold all weekend long, with a chili cook-off and barn dance to close the well attended event.

Farmers in the local area have been worried about the lack of precipitation this winter season, especially with the farming season quickly approaching. They're happy to see any rain or even snow at this point, which will help ensure good sources of irrigation water for fields and livestock. Last year at this time, the amount of precipitation was down 10% from the annual average, and currently it is down 15%.
